JOLLOF RICE

INGREDIENTS

For the Rice Base:

  • 3 cups of Jasmine or Basmati rice
  • 4-5 large tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 1 large onion, chopped (divided)
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2-3 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil

Spices and Seasonings

  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme (dried or fresh)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet or habanero peppers (whole, optional for heat)
  • 2 stock cubes (Maggi or knorr)
  • salt and black pepper to taste

Equipment Needed

  • Large heavy-bottomed pot with tight-fitting lid
  • Blender or food processor
  • Sharp knife and cutting board
  • Wooden spoon

INSTRUCTIONS

STEP 1: PREPARE THE TOMATO BASE

  1. Wash and rinse the rice until water runs clear. Set aside to drain.
  2. In a blender, combine chopped tomatoes, half of the chopped onion, garlic, and fresh ginger. blend until smooth to create a tomato puree.

STEP 2: COOK THE TOMATO BASE

  1. Heat vegetable oil in your large pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the remaining chopped onion and saut√® until translucent (about 3-4 minutes).
  3. Add tomato paste and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Pour in the blended tomato mixture and cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occassionally, until the liquid reduces and the mixture darkens to a deep red color.

STEP 3: SEASON THE BASE

  1. Add curry powder, thyme, smoked paprika, ground ginger, and garlic powder to the tomato base.
  2. Stir in the whole cubes, crushing them as you mix.
  3. Add the whole scotch bonnet pepper (don't break it if you want it mild)
  4. Season with salt and black pepper to taste

STEP 4: ADD RICE AND LIQUID

  1. Add the drained rice to the pot adn stir gently to coat each grain with the tomato mixture.
  2. Pour in 2 cups of stock initially, adding more as needed. The liquid should just cover the rice.
  3. Add bay leaves and bring the mixture to a boil.

STEP 5: COOK THE JOLLOF

  1. Once boiling, reduce heat to low and cover the pot with auliminum foil, then place the lid on top for a tight seal.
  2. Cook for 20-25 minutes without lifting the lid.
  3. After 20 minutes, check if rice is tender and liquid is absorbed. If rice needs more cooking, add a little more stock and cook for 5-10 more minutes.

STEP 6: FINAL STEPS

  1. Once rice is cooked, remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes.
  2. Remove bay leaves and scotch bonnet pepper.
  3. Gently stir in butter if using, and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  4. If adding vegetables, stir them in during the last 10 minutes of cooking.

FOR CHICKEN JOLLOF

  1. Season chicken pieces with salt, pepper, and some curry powder.
  2. Fry chicken separately until golden brown.
  3. Add to the pot during step 4 along with the rice.

SERVING SUGGESTIONS

Serve hot with:
  1. Fried plantains(kelewele)
  2. Coleslaw or simple salad
  3. Grilled or fried chicken
  4. Boiled eggs
  5. Avocado slices

TIPS FOR PERFECT JOLLOF

  1. "The Bottom pot:" Don't worry is some rice sticks to the bottom-this crispy layer(called "bottom pot") is considered a delicacy!
  2. Color is key: The rice should have a beautiful orange-red color from the tomatoes and spices.
  3. Don't over stir: Once you add the rice, minimize stirring to prevent it from becoming mushy.
  4. Liquid ratio: Start with less liquid and add more as needed. Different rice types absorb liquid differently.
  5. Patience: Let the tomato base cook down properly for the best flavor-this is the secret to great jollof!

STORAGE

  1. Refrigerate leftovers for up to 3 days.
  2. Reheat gently with a splash of water or stock.
  3. Can be frozen for up to 1 month.
